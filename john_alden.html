ABOUT:

Captain Johnathan "John" Alden is the main male protagonist of Salem. After the nine years war, he returns to Salem to find his hometown in a witch panic and that love of his life Mary is now married to George Sibley, the man who sent him off to war.
His father was often disappointed with John because instead of learning scripture or attending university, he chose to join the militia. When he met Mary Sibley, he quickly fell in love but later he was sent to fight the French and the Indians. He split a silver coin in half and gave one-half to Mary as a promise that he will come back to her within a year.
During the war, John was severally injured and left to die but was found by tribe men who brought him back. Shaman took care of John and let him live under his tend, teaching him their language and ways. While he and other men were out hunting, the village was raided by white men. John hunted and killed all those responsible.

  • The character description for the third season is as follows: "Former war hero, John Alden is forced to bury his love, Mary Sibley. After being captured and dragged back to Salem, Alden agrees to train the militia, preparing them for the coming war."

  • Historically, John Alden escaped Salem after he was accused of witchcraft. He took refuge in Duxbury where he married Elizabeth Phillips Everill who gave him 14 children. He was also the one who wrote the account of the Salem Witch Trials.
