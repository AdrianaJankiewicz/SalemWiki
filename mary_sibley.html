ABOUT:

Mary Sibley is the female protagonist of the series Salem. She is the wife of George Sibley, a counselor and head of the selectmen, the lover of John Alden, as well as the most powerful witch. She got her powers after she sacrificed her unborn child to Satan.
She was born and raised in Salem, Massachusetts. At some point in her childhood, her parents bought a slave girl Tituba who quickly forged a strong relationship with Mary, often treating each other like sisters.
Mary and young John Alden quickly fall in love, however, he is later sent to fight in the nine years war. He made a vow to Mary that he will return within a year and as a promise he gave her half of a silver coin. A few months later, she discovered she was pregnant with John's baby. Manipulated by witches, she believed that John died during the war and she sacrificed an unborn child to the Devil, becoming a witch herself. Sometime later, she married George Sibley after witches poisoned his previous wife. Within first years of their marriage, she placed her husband under a spell, wanting revenge on the man and leaving her in control of Salem.
